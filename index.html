The process

Questions produce constraints.
Constraints produce values.

Most design decisions are made by whoever has the strongest opinion that day. Derive replaces opinion with a systematic question set — every answer constrains the specification, every specification cites its research basis.

01 — Ask
The complete question set

Who is this for, right now, in their actual physiological state? What emotion do they arrive with? What do they need to leave with? What is the cognitive load budget? The time budget? The device reality? Every answer constrains what comes next.

→
02 — Research
1,991 indexed findings

Each answer queries the research database. What does the evidence say about this emotional state and color? This cognitive load and typography? This space type and acoustic coefficient? The database has the answer. With citations.

→
03 — Specify
No ambiguity. Ever.

The output is not "warm and approachable." The output is: background #F7F4EF, IBM Plex Sans 300 at 1.7 line-height, border-radius 2px, animation cubic-bezier(0.22, 1, 0.36, 1) 150ms. A builder who was never in the room can implement it exactly.

"Design decisions without research are just opinions with authority. The authority doesn't make them right. The research does."

The knowledge base

Built from evidence, not intuition.

The Derive research database covers every dimension of every design decision across every medium. Every finding is cited. Every value has a basis. Every conflict is documented.

150+ competitor brand analyses: exact color values, typography choices, emotional targets, what works, and — critically — what to avoid and the specific reason why.

Design decision log
Background #F7F4EF
dkb.color_research: warm backgrounds produce 67.6% reading comprehension vs 56.5% on cool/blue. Hue 38°, sat 22%, light 95%.
Accent #C4622D
dkb.emotion_to_color: authority + confidence states require warm, committed tones. Anti-pattern: blue accent (overused by Linear/Stripe/IBM — creates category confusion).
IBM Plex Sans body
dkb.font_alternatives: "Intelligent, human-centered, globally inclusive with enterprise authority." Carries institutional authority that Inter's neutrality lacks. Supports global translation.
0px container radius
dkb.emotion_design_matrix: authority states use 0px border radius. "Nothing here has been softened." 2px on interactive elements signals care without compromising authority.
Libre Franklin display
dkb.font_alternatives: "Classic American editorial, professional and versatile. Provides editorial heritage where Inter is deliberately neutral." 800 weight for authority signal.
